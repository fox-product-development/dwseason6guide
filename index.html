01

The Tile System

This is the single biggest change this season. There's no more free movement across the map — everything is built tile by tile, and it's the reason low-power players suddenly matter as much as whales.

  • A tile is created by sending an APC to occupy it — not crafted in a building.
  • You can only capture a tile adjacent to one you or your alliance already control.
  • To attack a city, that city must be connected to your tile network.
  • If the enemy recaptures a tile mid-line, everything past it is cut off until the connection is restored.

WHY THIS MATTERS
Everything past that lost tile is disconnected — unusable until it's retaken. This is exactly why alliances defend some "worthless" tiles more fiercely than the cities themselves. It's never about the tile — it's about what it keeps connected.
TILE LIMIT — CONFIRMED
Each player is capped at 40 tiles. Once you hit it, you release tiles or wait for space. Keep 30–40 useful tiles — don't burn your cap on dead-end positions.
02

Portals

Once your alliance controls enough connected territory (a 2×2 block), you can build a portal — instant APC movement and reinforcement to the front instead of marching in.

// BAD PLACEMENT
◎
Sits at the very tip of the advance — if the front falls back, it's isolated and becomes the first target.
// GOOD PLACEMENT
◎
Sits behind the front, protected by multiple access routes on either side.
03

Cities, Altars & Rewards

Cities generate Influence and drive the faction ranking. Higher-level cities — especially the central one — are worth far more, so factions concentrate on a handful of major targets instead of spreading thin.

Altars start appearing around Week 3, granting powerful alliance-wide skills — shields, missile-type attacks, and more. You can't hold every skill at once; which ones you pick shapes the alliance's entire strategy for the rest of the season.

War Merits are earned individually through captures, defence, and support. These unlock personal bonuses and end-of-season rewards on top of the faction ranking — so individual contribution still counts, even inside a team-first system.

How Alliance Roles Typically Split

On well-organised servers, alliances naturally divide into roles rather than every alliance doing everything.

Spearhead

  • Captures major cities
  • Leads the big battles
  • Holds the highest-value objectives

Support

  • Protects flanks
  • Reinforces the spearhead
  • Backfills losses on major fronts

Logistics / Route

  • Builds tile corridors
  • Captures oases
  • Defends key passages
  • Preps future offensives
OFTEN THE MOST DECISIVE ROLE
The logistics role isn't glamorous, but a fight is frequently won or lost entirely on whether the routes and oases behind the front line hold. Oases specifically produce Holy Water, used to upgrade your seasonal Obelisk and increase resistance — more oases under control means faster overall progress for everyone.

Route-Building Strategy

// CHOKEPOINT — one tile blocks an entire sector
◆

Hold it and you block a whole route. Lose it and the enemy opens an entirely new attack lane. The best strategists prioritise these before anything else.

// CROSSROADS — one tile, multiple directions
✛

Control this and you can launch offensives, cut off enemy movement, and defend more easily — all from a single position.

// SINGLE LINE
One lost tile in the middle stops everything, full stop.
// DOUBLE HIGHWAY
✕
Lose a tile on one line — the parallel line keeps the route alive.
07

Phase-by-Phase Buildout

1

Days 1–3 — Leave Spawn Fast

Don't try to grab every tile. Build one main corridor toward the nearest oases — that's your Holy Water and resistance source. Big accounts hold for major objectives; mid-level players secure oases and side routes.

2

Days 4–10 — First Cities Open

Don't build a single direct line to the city — one cut tile stops everything. Build a triangle: two converging routes, so if one side is cut, the other still supplies the city.

3

After Week 1 — Hub Formation

Major alliances build hubs — a central crossroads branching toward a city, an altar, and an oasis. Any one front can collapse without taking the whole position down.

4

Highways & Grids

Two main parallel routes, then cross-connections between them — often just called a "grid" by R5s.

5

Approaching the Centre — Build a Web

Stop building single routes entirely. At this stage no single tile loss can stop the advance — that's the whole point.

08

Player Discipline

A good player isn't just chasing kills. What R4s and R5s actually expect:

Captures the tiles they're asked to
Releases a tile immediately if an officer needs it for a better route
Stays available to recapture a lost tile fast
Follows the planned advance routes rather than freelancing
Capturing isolated tiles that connect to nothing
Filling the full 40-tile cap while the alliance still needs routes opened
Refusing to release a tile when an R4 asks
Attacking without checking the connection to the network is still live
FOR A TOP FINISH
Log in for every major opening — cities, altars, big battles. Keep stamina and speed-ups in reserve for war days rather than burning them early. Don't waste tile slots on dead positions — prioritise ones that open routes or guard key points. If you're not a top-power player, tile capture and defence is genuinely one of the most valuable things you can do this season.
